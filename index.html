Education

My professional journey began as a Graduate Teaching Assistant in the Industrial Engineering department at Jordan University of Science and Technology, where I served from 2018 to 2021. This role honed my teaching skills and deepened my understanding of industrial engineering principles. In 2022, I expanded my research horizons as a Graduate Research Assistant in the Engineering Management and Systems Engineering department at Old Dominion University, Norfolk, VA, USA. Since 2023, I have been contributing to cutting-edge research as a Research Assistant in the IST, School of Modeling, Simulation, and Training at the University of Central Florida.

I have authored three peer-reviewed journal articles and seven conference papers, reflecting my commitment to advancing knowledge in my field. My research interests encompass Operations Research, Digital Twins, Human-Centered Engineering, Computer Vision, and Supply Chain Logistics.

My career objective is to leverage my expertise in industrial engineering with a focus on Modeling and Simulation to develop innovative solutions that drive technological advancements and optimize complex systems. I believe in a collaborative and interdisciplinary approach to research and problem-solving, aiming to make a meaningful impact in both academia and industry.
